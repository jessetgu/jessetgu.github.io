Elasticity of hydrous rhyolitic glasses


Glasses are commonly used as analogs of melts in Earth's interior. Here, we use Brillouin Light Scattering to measure sound velocities (VP and VS) and FTIR spectroscopy to measure water concentrations in rhyolitic glasses as functions of pressure. We find that hydrous glasses become as incompressible as their anhydrous counterpart above ~1.5 GPa, causing their velocities to converge at high pressure. Our results may provide some insight into the behavior of hydrous melts in felsic magma chambers at depth.
